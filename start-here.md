# Start Here

## The Basic Question

Why should the country someone is born in determine what their work is worth?

That question is the starting point for Global Equality Lab.

Around the world, people with similar effort, talent, and skill often live radically different economic lives. A person born in a wealthy country may have access to better schools, safer institutions, stronger passports, richer customers, trusted banking systems, and employers who can pay far more. A person born somewhere else may work just as hard and still face much lower wages and fewer chances.

This is not because people in one country matter more than people in another.

## The Simple Dream

The simple dream is fair economic opportunity across borders.

That does not mean everyone has the same job, the same life, or the same income. It means birthplace should have less power to decide whether a person's work can support a safe, dignified, and hopeful life.

If talent is global, opportunity should be less trapped by geography.

## The Hard Reality

The answer is not simple.

Wages are shaped by many things: local prices, infrastructure, trust, legal systems, business networks, education, trade rules, migration rules, currency, and the power employers have over workers. You cannot fix all of that by declaring that every person should be paid the same.

Some ideas sound fair but cannot scale. Some ideas help a small group while leaving the larger system untouched. Some ideas depend too much on wealthy employers doing the right thing. Some may be weakened by AI, automation, politics, or bad incentives.

That is why this lab exists.

## What This Lab Is Doing

Global Equality Lab is comparing possible ways to reduce global economic inequality.

The lab asks:

- Which ideas could improve real incomes or opportunity across borders?
- Which ideas could scale beyond a small pilot?
- Which ideas could survive criticism from experts and affected communities?
- Which ideas could become the foundation for a real organization?
- Which ideas should be abandoned because they do not hold up?

The goal is not to publish impressive essays forever. The goal is to decide what organization should actually be founded.

## How Your Input Can Help

Public feedback can change the project.

You can help by:

- pointing out a false assumption
- sharing lived experience
- submitting evidence
- naming a better approach
- challenging the current leading idea
- suggesting a small test or pilot
- explaining why an idea would fail in practice

Disagreement is welcome. The lab is trying to find weak points early, before time and money are spent building the wrong thing.
