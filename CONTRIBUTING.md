# How to Contribute

Global Equality Lab is a public strategy process for deciding what organization should be founded to reduce global economic inequality.

You do not need permission to challenge the project. Serious disagreement is welcome.

## You Do Not Need To Be An Expert

You can contribute if you have lived experience, professional knowledge, research skill, building skill, funding interest, hiring experience, policy experience, or a careful question.

The lab needs many kinds of input because global inequality is not only an economics problem. It is also a work problem, a power problem, a trust problem, a technology problem, and a lived human problem.

## Ways To Contribute

- Challenge a claim in the strategy documents.
- Suggest a better approach.
- Submit evidence.
- Share lived experience about cross-border work, hiring, migration, or opportunity.
- Propose a pilot that could test an idea quickly.
- Score a strategy using the [Strategy Scoring Rubric](/scoring-rubric).
- Add a debate entry or improve an existing one.
- Help make the public pages clearer.

## What Makes A Strong Contribution

A strong contribution explains how the world works and what follows from that.

Strong contributions usually include:

- a clear claim
- evidence, experience, or reasoning
- a mechanism that explains cause and effect
- a serious failure mode
- what would change your mind

## What Makes A Weak Contribution

A weak contribution may still be useful, but it is harder to act on.

Weak contributions often:

- only say an idea is good or bad
- rely on slogans
- ignore tradeoffs
- assume good intentions are enough
- avoid the hardest objection
- propose a solution without explaining how it scales

Use this structure when you can:

- Claim:
- Evidence or experience:
- Mechanism:
- Why this might fail:
- What would change your mind:

## How Comments Become Part Of The Project

Markdown files are the canonical source for the project.

GitHub Discussions are the public conversation layer. Strong comments may become issues, pull requests, or edits to the strategy documents.

## Practical Notes

- Use plain English when writing for the public pages.
- Technical language is welcome on expert pages when it adds precision.
- If you use AI to help, include enough detail that a human can inspect the reasoning.
- Disagreement is useful when it is specific.
