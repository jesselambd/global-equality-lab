# Current Debate Queue

These are the priority questions Global Equality Lab needs to answer before choosing what organization to found.

They are not side issues. They are the work.

## Priority Questions

1. Is remote fair hiring structurally vulnerable to AI automation?
2. Should the organization become a talent marketplace, a certification body, an auditor, or a policy/movement lab?
3. Is ownership a stronger lever than labor income?
4. What mechanism could scale without depending on Western employer goodwill?
5. What would produce measurable income gains within 12 months?
6. What would still matter if AI collapses the price of white-collar digital labor?
7. What is the smallest credible pilot?
8. What metric should replace total income moved?
9. What would make us abandon the current leading idea?
10. What kind of organization should actually be founded?

## How To Answer A Question

Useful answers should include:

- a clear claim
- a mechanism
- evidence or experience
- the strongest objection
- what would change your mind
- a suggested next experiment, if possible

## Current Bias To Test

The lab currently treats fair-paid transparent remote hiring as the leading hypothesis. That may be wrong.

The best contributions will either strengthen the idea by surviving criticism or replace it with something better.
